-------------------------------------------------------------
1. ANNUAL LEAVE POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
All full-time employees are entitled to 25 days of annual leave per year.
Part-time employees receive annual leave on a pro-rata basis.
Leave must be approved by line managers at least 2 weeks in advance.
During peak business periods (December and August), leave requests require
4 weeks advance notice and are subject to team availability.
Unused leave can be carried over up to a maximum of 5 days per year.
Carried over leave must be used within the first quarter (Q1) of the new year
or it will be forfeited.
Employees may not take more than 15 consecutive working days of leave
without prior written approval from their department head.
Leave balances are visible on the HR self-service portal at all times.

-------------------------------------------------------------
2. REMOTE WORK POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week.
The remaining 2 days must be spent in the office unless otherwise agreed.
Core hours are 10am to 3pm where all employees must be available online
and responsive to communications regardless of work location.
Remote work arrangements must be agreed with the line manager in writing.
Employees working remotely are responsible for maintaining a safe,
professional, and distraction-free work environment.
The company will provide a one-time home office equipment allowance of
up to £500 for eligible employees working remotely.
Remote work from outside the country of employment requires prior approval
from both the line manager and the HR department, and must not exceed
4 weeks per calendar year for tax and compliance reasons.
Employees on probation are not eligible for remote work during their
first 3 months of employment.

-------------------------------------------------------------
3. PROBATION POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
All new employees are subject to a probation period of 6 months.
During probation, performance will be reviewed at the 1-month,
3-month, and 6-month marks.
Employees on probation are not eligible for remote work, bonus schemes,
or enhanced benefits until probation is successfully completed.
Either party may terminate employment during probation with 2 weeks notice.
Upon successful completion of probation, the employee moves to the standard
notice period as defined in their contract.
Line managers must submit a probation completion form to HR at least
2 weeks before the probation end date.

-------------------------------------------------------------
4. LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
Every employee is entitled to a personal Learning and Development (L&D)
budget of £1,500 per year.
The L&D budget can be used for external courses, certifications,
conferences, books, and online learning platforms.
All L&D spend must be pre-approved by the line manager before booking.
Employees must submit a learning request form via the HR portal.
If the cost of a course exceeds £500, a post-completion learning summary
must be submitted to the line manager within 30 days.
Mandatory compliance training (including GDPR, Health and Safety, and
Information Security) must be completed within 30 days of joining and
renewed annually. Failure to complete mandatory training may result in
disciplinary action.
The company uses Cornerstone OnDemand (CSOD) as its Learning Management
System (LMS). All training records are maintained on the LMS.
Employees wishing to pursue a formal qualification (degree, masters, or
professional certification) may apply for study leave of up to 3 days
per year subject to manager approval.

-------------------------------------------------------------
5. PERFORMANCE REVIEW POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: in June and December.
All employees must complete a self-assessment form at least one week
before their scheduled review meeting.
Performance is rated on a five-point scale:
  1 - Below expectations
  2 - Partially meets expectations
  3 - Meets expectations
  4 - Exceeds expectations
  5 - Outstanding
Employees rated 1 or 2 will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan
(PIP) within 30 days of the review.
A PIP typically runs for 60 to 90 days and includes specific measurable
targets, regular check-ins, and HR involvement.
Employees rated 4 or 5 are eligible for accelerated career progression
discussions and may be considered for spot bonuses.
All review documentation must be submitted to HR within 5 business days
of the review meeting.

-------------------------------------------------------------
6. COMPENSATION AND BONUS POLICY
-------------------------------------------------------------
Salaries are reviewed annually in January, effective from 1st February.
Salary increases are based on performance rating, market benchmarking,
and budget availability.
Employees must have completed their probation period to be eligible
for a salary review.
The company operates a discretionary annual bonus scheme.
Bonus eligibility requires a minimum performance rating of 3 (meets
expectations) in the most recent review cycle.
Bonus payments are made in March each year for the previous financial year.
Employees who leave the company before the bonus payment date are not
eligible to receive a bonus unless otherwise stated in their contract.
All compensation information is strictly confidential.
