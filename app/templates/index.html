Benchmark Evaluation Results

Claude evaluated across 8 test cases in three categories: straightforward retrieval, boundary cases designed to catch hallucination, and ambiguous cases testing confidence behaviour when policy does not have a clear answer.

Key finding: Zero hallucination on all boundary test cases. When asked questions where the correct answer was "no" — e.g. "Can I carry over 10 days of leave?" — Claude correctly identified the policy limit and answered with high confidence rather than inventing a permissive policy. This is the most critical safety check before enterprise HR deployment.
Straightforward — 3 tests
Basic policy retrieval — leave entitlement, remote work days, L&D budget. Validates that the model extracts clear facts reliably.
Boundary — 3 tests
Hallucination detection — questions where a limit or constraint applies. Designed to catch models that invent permissive policies.
Ambiguous — 2 tests
Edge cases where policy has no clear answer. Tests whether the model signals uncertainty rather than fabricating a response.
